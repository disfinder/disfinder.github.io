"Set up a VPN home" has been sitting in my to-do list for a long time.
<!--more-->
It settled in and had no intention of going anywhere — first nobody left the house during the pandemic, then more urgent tasks kept showing up.
And then someone in a chat reminded me of a service I had already heard of before, and heard only good things about — [Tailscale](https://tailscale.com/)

In short, for home use — it's kind of like a successor (and an improvement) to Hamachi LogMeIn, if anyone still remembers that. The author of Tailscale was inspired by Hamachi and wanted to get rid of its shortcomings — and by his own account, succeeded.

Haven't tried adding anyone to my networks yet, but by installing their app on a Synology, MacBook, iPad, and iPhone — it immediately solved several remote access problems:

- access to shared folders on the MacBook
- ~~backup~~ photo backup from my phone to the Synology
- watching videos via Plex on the tablet

Well, with a minimum of effort everything works out of the box and covers my needs to a fair degree.
We'll see how it goes from here.
