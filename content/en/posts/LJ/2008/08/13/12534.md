Not long ago I was this handsome, self-confident guy standing firmly on both legs.

[![](http://s46.radikal.ru/i112/0808/c3/2cf4412e9207t.jpg)](http://s46.radikal.ru/i112/0808/c3/2cf4412e9207.jpg) A small observation: with a broken arm you still have three limbs left for getting around. You can walk on two legs and carry a cup of tea in your hand, for example.

[![](http://s59.radikal.ru/i164/0808/d0/6fac2f2f1585t.jpg)](http://s59.radikal.ru/i164/0808/d0/6fac2f2f1585.jpg)
But when it's a broken leg and you have to get around on crutches — you can forget about bringing anything anywhere. I'm starting to envy kangaroos — could just toss a thermos and a sandwich in the pouch and hop from the fridge to the computer.

[![](http://s58.radikal.ru/i160/0808/54/3b0d679c15fat.jpg)](http://radikal.ru/F/s58.radikal.ru/i160/0808/54/3b0d679c15fa.jpg.html)
This is the workstation setup I put together for myself. Still uncomfortable — the leg gets tired, the neck hurts. But I haven't figured out yet how to lie down and browse the internet at the same time. I mean, thoughts of a 40" panel on the ceiling did cross my mind, but I need something less expensive.
